A board move takes the form of an u16
formats:
    abbb bbbc cccc cd00
castling format:
    abc* **** **** ****
first byte (a):
    0 - normal move/ en passant
    1 - castle
    if normal or en passant:
        bbbbbb (0-63) describes from square
        cccccc describes dest square
        d describes if move is en passant
    if castle:
        b describes color (0 - black, 1 - white)
        c describes side (0 - queen, 1 - king)

A board takes the form of an u256
piece codes:
    000 - blank
    001 - pawn
    010 - knight
    100 - bishop
    101 - rook
    110 - queen
    111 - king
this will take 3*64=192 bits
193rd byte describes color (0 - black, 1 - white)
194-197 describes castling rights:
    194 - w queen
    195 - w king
    196 - b queen
    197 - b king
198-204 describes en passant target square:
    if 198th byte is 1 then en passant is possible
    else ignore
205-211 describes halfmove clock
    ranges from 0 - 50
    if it is move 49 and black makes an half move
    declare an draw
